Diabetes ML Evaluation

When external validation exposes the deployment shift that strong internal cross-validation hides entirely.

1.28MExternal patients (BRFSS)
−9.7%Relative AUC decline
5-foldNested cross-validation
SHAP+ subgroup analysis

The Problem

Strong internal validation does not predict external performance under cohort shift. A model that looks excellent on a held-out split of its own training population can silently lose accuracy the moment it meets a different cohort — and standard reporting never surfaces it.

Approach

Models are tuned with nested 5-fold cross-validation on the source cohort, then evaluated unchanged on 1.28M BRFSS respondents — a genuinely external population. SHAP attribution and stratified subgroup analysis trace where and for whom the degradation concentrates.

Outcome

External AUC falls −9.7% relative to the internal estimate — a deployment shift entirely invisible to the internal cross-validation that reported strong, stable performance. The lesson generalises: external validation is not an optional extra, it is the only honest measure of clinical readiness.
