Persistent Racial Disparities

Five independent identification strategies, one conclusion: the racial approval gap in U.S. mortgage lending survives every robustness check.

42MHMDA applications
5Identification strategies
>9 ppManski lower bound
2020–24Coverage window

The Problem

Raw approval gaps conflate creditworthiness with discrimination. A single regression can always be dismissed as an omitted-variable artifact — so the real question is whether the unexplained component survives when the identification assumptions are stressed from five different directions.

Approach

The headline gap is stress-tested with DiNardo–Fortin–Lemieux decomposition, within-lender fixed effects, regression discontinuity at the PMI threshold, difference-in-differences across the 2022–24 credit tightening, and Manski partial-identification bounds that make no functional-form assumption at all.

Outcome

The gap persists under every method. Even the worst-case Manski bound — which assumes maximally adversarial selection on unobservables — leaves a >9 pp lower bound. When five methods with different failure modes all agree, the result is no longer an artifact of any one of them.
