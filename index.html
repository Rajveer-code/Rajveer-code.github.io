01

Thesis

One question, pursued across lending, medicine, language, and markets.

Every consequential system is a decision system. A mortgage model decides who builds wealth. A clinical model decides who gets treated. A language model decides whose words are believed. My research asks who benefits, who is harmed, where these systems fail — and proves, with causal evidence at scale, how they can be improved.

Persistent Racial Disparities in U.S. Mortgage Approval

Evidence from 42 Million Applications, 2020–2024

A full-scale empirical study of racial approval gaps in U.S. mortgage markets using HMDA microdata and five quasi-experimental identification strategies — stacking DFL decomposition, within-lender fixed effects, RDD at the 80% LTV threshold, DiD during the 2022–2024 tightening cycle, and Manski bounds.
